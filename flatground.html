Flatground Tricks

Getting Comfortable

Before attempting tricks, it's good practice to first get comfortable on your skateboard. Learning these will help you to get your balance down:

  • Riding, Turning and Cruising
  • Manual
  • Nose Manual
  • Pushing Switch

The Basics

  • Ollie
  • BS Pop Shove It
  • FS Pop Shove it
  • BS 180
  • FS 180
  • Kickflip
  • Heelflip

Basic Combinations

  • BS Bigspin = BS Pop Shove It + BS 180
  • Pop Shuv Body Varial = BS Pop Shove It + FS 180
  • FS Bigspin = FS Shove It + FS 180
  • Front Shuv Body Varial = FS Shove It + BS 180
  • BS Flip = Kickflip + BS 180
  • FS Flip = Kickflip + FS 180
  • BS Heel = Heelflip + BS 180
  • FS Heel = Heelflip + FS 180

Doing Tricks in Different Stances

All of the above tricks can be done in different stances.

If your left foot is your front foot on your skateboard, this means you are riding regular. If your right foot is your front foot on your skateboard, you are riding goofy.

If you ride with your front foot on your nose, this means you are riding "Nollie". When you ride backwards in your normal stance, you are riding "Fakie". Riding in the opposite stance is called "Switch".

This means that whenever you do a trick in the opposite stance, you are doing that trick switch.

If you ride backwards while doing an Ollie, you've successfully accomplished a "Fakie Ollie". Riding in your normal stance with your front foot on the board, you could pop a "Nollie Front Shuv". Put together, these four stances (normal, fakie, switch, nollie) allow for endless trick combinations!
